Espalier · Release notes
1.1.0
Jul 2026
Boards as tabs, and a bench that behaves.
New
·Your boards are tabs. Boards sit on the titlebar now — click to switch, + for a new one, right-click for Rename, Export, Reveal, and Remove. The sidebar is retired and the canvas gets the whole window.
·A new icon and calmer chrome. An icon that follows macOS’s Icon & widget style, including dark, and one calm titlebar matching the rest of the suite.
Fixed
·Cards stay put. A column growing to receive a drop could quietly claim a neighbouring card, and reopening a board could pile untouched cards in the corner. Placed is placed now.
·The bench really is endless. Dragging a card to the window’s edge scrolls the bench with it, and you can always scroll a full screen past your farthest card.
1.0.2
Jun 2026
Sharper, faster, easier to learn.
New
·Expand any card. Click a card’s body to open it full-size for focused writing, with a quick color picker right on the card.
·Templates & a welcome board. Every template now opens with example cards, and a new Welcome board walks you through the basics (revisit it from the Help menu).
·Choose your editor font. Pick the writing font you like in Settings → Editor.
Improved
·Columns & stacks. Reorder cards inside a column, fan out a stack, and move a whole column — notes and all — to another board.
·Much faster on big boards. Scrolling large boards is now smooth, with off-screen cards culled as you pan.
1.0.1
Jun 2026
Runs anywhere, no setup.
Fixed
·No developer tools needed. Board history now works on any Mac out of the box, with no command-line tools to install.
1.0.0
Jun 2026
Welcome to Espalier.
New
·A spatial board. Arrange cards on a pannable dot-grid surface. Drag them freely, or snap them into labeled columns and rows.
·Stacks and containers. Gather cards into stacks, or drop them into columns and rows that auto-size and clean up without overlaps.
·Grows from Seed. Pull ripe ideas from a Seed garden onto the board as cards, then shape them into sequence.
·Export anywhere. Send a board out as Markdown, PDF, CSV, OPML, or plain text.
