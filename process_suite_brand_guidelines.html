The Process Suite

Tools for making, tracking, and showing work.
01 — naming & positioning

The Suite

Four apps. One design language. Each does a distinct job in a creative workflow — capturing, outlining, archiving, presenting — and can be used alone or together. Ideas begin in Seed, take structure in Scene Board, are documented in Provenance, and presented in Works. The suite name is "Process Suite" in formal contexts. Individual apps are always referred to by their single-word name only.

Seed
Where ideas take root.
Local-first idea capture. Fragments, thoughts, dialogue, images. The beginning of everything.
--seed-accent: #C87B5A
Scene Board
Structure before you shoot.
Project outliner and kanban board. Cards, scenes, sequences. Organize work before it exists.
--scene-accent: #B89B6A
Provenance
The work remembers.
Process history and version tracker. Show proof of work, document decisions, branch ideas.
--prov-accent: #1D9E75
Works
Present what you've made.
Portfolio formatter. Context, format, hierarchy. Show finished work the way it deserves to be seen.
--works-accent: #5A6480
02 — icon system

App Icons

Each app has a geometric mark derived from its core function. All four share the same superellipse container, gradient treatment, and edge stroke — making them family at any size. The mark uses parchment white (#FAF6EF) at stepped opacity levels to convey depth and hierarchy without color.

05 — spacing & shape

Layout Tokens

Consistent radii and spacing make the apps feel unified even when opened side by side. The small radii are intentional — they reference the measured restraint of early Apple software.

--radius-sm3px
--radius-md6px
--radius-lg10px
--radius-pill999px
--space-xs4px
--space-sm8px
--space-md12px
--space-lg20px
--space-xl32px
--border-ui0.5px solid tan-200

No drop shadows. No gradients in UI chrome. Borders are always 0.5px. Elevation is expressed through background color difference, not shadow.

06 — brand moments

Splash & Marketing

Splash screens, about pages, and marketing surfaces use Playfair Display on the dark slate background. Keep it spare — the brand font does the work.
