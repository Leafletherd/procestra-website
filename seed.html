The garden
Every note is a seed in a folder on your disk. Plain text files, nothing more. Searchable. No folders required. They grow until they're something.
The bench
A free-form canvas. Drag seeds out, arrange them as cards, stack them in columns and rows, or leave them loose. Structure is optional.

The garden
Ideas before they're ideas.

A seed is anything: a title, a fragment, a question you can't answer yet. It lives in the garden as a plain text file in a folder you choose — not in the cloud, not behind a login.

Seed tags itself. A Sprout is a new idea. Growing means you've been at it. Rooted means the note has real weight — words, depth, something to work with. Dormant means you haven't touched it in a while. None of these are categories you assign. They're signals the garden sends back. The whole system is designed for one thing: forward momentum.

The bench
Where you think out loud.

Drag a seed from the garden and it becomes a card. Move cards anywhere on the bench — loose on the surface, grouped in columns, arranged in rows, or stacked when space is short. Drag a whole group. Break it apart. The bench doesn't impose a shape. It lets you find one.

Cards can be merged into a single seed. When the bench has given you a shape worth writing from, transplant it — columns become outline sections, cards become entries. The structure travels; the seeds stay in the garden.

Pairs with Provenance

Ideas travel.

When an idea becomes a project, the original note travels with it. Connect a project in Provenance and it can trace back to the seed it came from — the first draft, the timestamp, the beginning of the record. If an idea started in Seed before becoming a project, Provenance shows the original source. The full story of how a project came to be.
